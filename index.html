About Us

Rice University and Texas Medical Center (TMC) are in a unique position to deliver the key fundamental innovations that will drive the discovery of next-generation cancer medicine. To catalyze the generation of innovative drugs and technologies that can improve the diagnostics and therapeutics of human diseases, exploration of the fundamental aspects of underlying chemical, biological and physical processes and connecting them better for therapy are important. With strong foundations in theoretical biophysics, molecular design, nanotechnology, biological sciences, and engineering, Rice is able to deliver the evolution of therapeutics at the atomic, molecular, and nanometer levels, which is not currently available to the adjacent Texas Medical Center. To translate our knowledge for cancer, Baylor College of Medicine, Texas Children’s Hospital are committed to the study of breast health and the development of cancer treatment. Rice and TMC can push the boundaries of clinical research across an extensive network of different institutions and pioneer effective health policy solutions to address the complex cancer problems of today.


What is Our Goal?

Catalyze the generation of innovative drugs and technologies. SICC can improve the diagnostics and therapeutics of human diseases by exploring the fundamental aspects of organic chemistry, chemical biology, and nanomaterial synthesis, and connecting them better to investigated health phenomena. Foster collaborations between Rice and TMC. With strong foundations in organic chemistry, chemical biology, nanotechnology, biological sciences, and engineering, Rice is in a unique position to deliver the key fundamental innovations that will drive the biomedical industry forward. SICC is able to deliver the evolution of synthetic therapeutics at the atomic, molecular, and nanometer levels to colleagues at TMC, which is not currently available to the adjacent TMC. This effort will provide opportunities for both Rice and TMC researchers to apply for high-level NIH/DoD/CPRIT/NSF research and training grants, especially the multi-PI grants and center grants
